EDA On The United States Air Pollution Air Quality Index (AQI)

Air pollution is a significant issue that can affect public health, ecosystem health, and climate change. In the US, the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) measures air quality using the Air Quality Index (AQI), which is a numerical value calculated from the concentration of different pollutants in the air. The AQI provides an indicator of how clean or polluted the air is and its potential health effects.

My objective for this project was to perform exploratory data analysis on US air pollution AQI data and gain some insights into the trends, patterns, and correlations between AQI and different pollutants across the United States between the years 2000 to 2021. The data used in this project was originally collected from the EPA's Air Quality System (AQS) database and was sourced from a dataset created on Kaggle.com.

The project involved the following steps:

  1. Data acquisition: The project will aquire the AQI and pollutant data from a dataset from Kaggle.com, which was originally sourced from the AQS database.
  2. Data explorationg: The project will utilize Tableau to create interactive visualizations that allow for in-depth analysis of trends, patterns, and outliers
  3. Data analysis: The project will analyze correlations between the AQI and different pollutants, like CO, NO2, SO2, and O3, across the United States using statistical methods such as correlation analysis and time-series analysis through Python and Pandas.
  4. Visualization: The project will include interactive visualizations such as line charts, heatmaps, and maps using Tableau to communicate the findings and insights from data analysis.
  5. Conclusion: The project will include a summary of the findings, insights, and limitations of the analysis and provide recommendations for further research improvements.

Tableau Dashboard

The Tableau dashboard for this project features interactive maps of four different air pollutants: CO AQI, NO2 AQI, O3 AQI, and SO2 AQI. The maps showcase the AQI levels for each pollutant across the United States from 2000 to 2021. Users can hover over different regions to see the specific AQI level for that pollutant and year. Additionally, the dashboard includes a side-by-side bar graph that shows the measured levels of each pollutant. This graph allows for easy comparison of the different pollutants and their trends over time. AQI is measured on a scale from 0-500. 0-50 is good, 51-100 is moderate, and any values over 100 are generally unhealthy or can be hazardous to health. Overall, the trends show that AQI for the United States is between 0-80, which is at the level of good/moderate. O3 levels have slightly increased over the years, while NO2, CO, and SO2 levels are decreasing.
